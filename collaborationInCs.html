Collaboration in Computer Science

On the job, computer scientists and programmers work as part of a team to plan, develop, and launch their products. In this context, they must know how to consider the diverse perspectives of teammates and outside stakeholders, and weight them as much as their own. This course identifies the practices of collaboration and inclusion that are integral to meaningful computer science and coding education, how teachers can implement those practices in instruction, and the implications these practices have beyond the computer science and coding world.

What does collaboration look like in Computer Science?

The myth that coders and other Computer Science professionals work as individuals is just that - a myth. Coders and other Computer Science professionals typically work in teams. In many ways, collaboration in Computer Science looks like collaboration in other disciplines - that said, notable differences exist.

More information on collaboration in Computer Science can be found in the accompanying deck (located on the resources sidebar).

Role assignment in Computer Science projects

In all disciplines, group roles and dynamics are critical to effective collaboration. In Computer Science, considerations such as access to technology make these dynamics even more crucial.

Assignment - Using the provided project overview:

  • Design group structure
  • Describe group roles including title, responsibilities, and topic ownership

Structured collaboration

Given the nature of Computer Science projects, certain collaboration structures and checkpoints must be incorporated into project timelines. These include peer review, iteration, and quality control.

Assignment - Using the provided project overview:

  • Build project timeline
  • Identify checkpoints for peer review including instructions for each group member
  • Identify checkpoints for iteration including instructions for each group member
  • Identify checkpoints for quality control including instructions for each group member
